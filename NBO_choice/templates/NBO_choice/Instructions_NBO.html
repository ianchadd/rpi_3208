At the beginning of each Decision Problem, the computer program will randomly decide between two courses of action:
1. Show you {{Constants.num_options}} options, each with {{Constants.num_attributes}} characters (i.e. #'s and %'s). You can then earn points by choosing from those options.
2. Give you an outside option that earns you {{Constants.nbo_value}} points and skip showing you any displayed options.

These two courses of action are equally likely to occur. More specifically, each happens with 50% probability.

This outside option has no display and earns you {{Constants.nbo_value}} points if it is given to you. Giving you the outside option effectively means that the computer program is forcing you to skip the Decision Problem, opting to give you {{Constants.nbo_value}} points instead of having you earn them by selecting a displayed option.

This will happen in every Decision Problem. So if you are given the outside option in one Decision Problem, you may still be able to choose from displayed options in a later Decision Problem.
